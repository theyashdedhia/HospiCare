Long Term Care
Long term nursing service provides comprehensive healthcare to individuals who require extended care due to chronic conditions. These services are typically provided by trained nurses in the individual's home or in a healthcare facility. The care can include medication management, wound care, monitoring vital signs, assistance with daily living activities, and emotional support. The goal of long term nursing care is to improve the quality of life of individuals who require ongoing care and support, while also ensuring that they receive proper medical attention and attention to their specific needs.
Wound Dressing
Wound dressing service provides skilled care for wound management. Trained professionals offer dressing changes, infection control, and treatment recommendations. The service is important for patients with acute or chronic wounds, to promote healing and prevent infections. Proper wound dressing can also help control pain and discomfort, and minimize scarring. Wound dressing service may be provided in hospitals, long-term care facilities, or in-home care settings.
Catheterization
Catheterization service involves the insertion, maintenance, and removal of bladder catheters by trained healthcare professionals. This service is important for patients who require temporary or permanent catheterization due to urinary tract disorders or other medical conditions. Proper catheterization helps prevent infection, manage urinary incontinence, and improve overall patient comfort. Catheterization service may be provided in hospitals, clinics, or in-home care settings.
IV Infusion
IV infusion service involves the safe and accurate administration of intravenous medications, fluids, and nutrients by healthcare professionals. This service is important for patients who require medication that cannot be taken orally, such as chemotherapy or antibiotics. IV infusion can also be used to provide hydration or nutrition to patients who are unable to take these substances orally. Healthcare professionals monitor the patient's vital signs and response to the medication, ensuring safe and effective treatment. IV infusion service may be provided in hospitals, clinics, or in-home care settings.
